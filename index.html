How the Quiz Works

Drawing on trusted resources like eBird and Audubon, this quiz invites you to explore the amazing diversity of birds around us. Questions highlight identification tips, behavior, habitat, migration, and conservation stories from across the globe.

We publish fresh quiz content every two weeks: new regions to explore, spotlights on rare species, and features that highlight endangered birds and the threats they face. As you test your skills and learn new facts, you’ll also see practical ways to help—log your sightings on eBird, connect with local Audubon chapters, and support conservation partners.

The quiz is free to use. When Pi donations become supported by bird conservation nonprofits, you’ll be able to contribute directly to bird protection — and optionally support ongoing development of this project.

My commitment is that once verified non-profit bird conservation organizations begin accepting Pi, future Pi contributions made through this app will be donated directly to KYB-approved groups. Any remaining portion will support ongoing development and maintenance of the project.

This app is about action, not just answers. Together we can understand, appreciate, and protect our feathered friends—one question at a time. With millions of potential birders among Pi Network Pioneers, we have a unique chance to bring Pi cryptocurrency to the forefront of global bird conservation.

How to Play

  1. Start the quiz and identify as many birds as you can.
  2. Click on a bird name to select your answer.
  3. At the end of the quiz, review your results and learn more about each species.
  4. Enjoy discovering new birds and expanding your knowledge!

Why It Matters

Bird populations are under pressure—habitat loss, collisions, invasive species, and climate change all play a role. Studies estimate North America alone has lost roughly 3 billion birds since 1970. BirdID-Lite’s mission is to help people learn and care—and to support the groups doing the hard work on the ground.

Pledge: BirdID‑Lite will support bird conservation efforts once verified nonprofits begin accepting Pi. When that becomes possible, a portion of all Pi contributions will be directed toward organizations that have completed KYB (Know Your Business) on the Pi platform.
